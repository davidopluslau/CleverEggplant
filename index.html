The Clever Eggplant is David's bartending project, (typically) an informal bar at StartX, which is Stanford's startup incubator.

In May 2014, David, a newly employed software developer, got pulled into one of Weeby's weekly game nights at StartX. There, he started volunteering as their for the next year. Afterward, he worked as a bartender at the Mountain View restaurant Bushido Izakaya.

At the start of this summer, Ivy finished her first year of her master's degree at Duke and came to LinkedIn as a software development intern. When asked by the company about her interests, she broadcasted her developing curiosity about cocktails. David saw this and met with her, eventually spending the following two months teaching her everything he knew about bartending.

This summer has just about come to its end; David has nothing left to teach Ivy. Tonight is at once a test of Ivy's growth, a showcase of her abilities, and a celebration to conclude a great season.

Why are we The Clever Eggplant?

The bar was nameless for its first six months of operation. Our name reflects our origins as a supporting fixture of StartX's board game nights. One night, a few of us were playing Apples to Apples. David played the green card "Clever", prompting the other players to choose cards that they thought were evocative of cleverness. One of his friends had no good answers and played "Eggplant" in response to get rid of an otherwise useless card. The ludicrous combination of those cards amused David so much that he made it the name of the bar.

How did David start bartending?

His mixing began when he received a mixology book for his birthday. He likes to say that reading the book and watching Youtube videos accounts for 10% of his skill and knowledge, while the other 90% comes from patrons at the bar bombarding him with drink requests until he could handle them all.

Thank you for your generosity and patronage!
